# Evidence and Branding Standard

## Purpose

Every lab should produce proof of capability, not just notes.

The evidence should help with:

* interviews
* CV updates
* LinkedIn posts
* recruiter conversations
* mentoring conversations
* self-review

## Minimum Evidence Standard

Each completed lab should include:

* what was attempted
* why it mattered
* what evidence was captured
* what decisions were made
* what risks or limitations were identified
* what could be improved in production
* what career story the lab supports

## Career Leverage Outputs

Each lab should produce at least two of the following:

| Output | Purpose |
| ------ | ------- |
| STAR story | Interview preparation |
| Recruiter summary | Clear value statement |
| LinkedIn post idea | Public visibility |
| Portfolio note | Long-term evidence |
| Conversation script | Social and communication practice |
| CV bullet | Application material |

## Tone Standard

The tone should be:

* clear
* accurate
* evidence-based
* not exaggerated
* not apologetic
* not vague

## Example Recruiter-Facing Summary

> I practised a realistic support scenario involving macOS access troubleshooting across identity, network, and device configuration layers. The lab produced technical evidence, user communication, and an interview-ready explanation of the troubleshooting approach.

## Example LinkedIn Post Structure

```text
Today I worked on [topic].

The main technical lesson was [lesson].

The professional lesson was [communication, ownership, or decision-making lesson].

This matters because [production or career relevance].
```
